Kina
Product Vision
Overview

Kina is a web application designed to facilitate meaningful in-person conversations during social meetups. Instead of encouraging people to spend more time on their phones, Kina acts as a lightweight event companion that guides participants through structured activities before quietly stepping into the background.

Participants join a meetup by scanning a QR code, entering their name, and following a series of guided conversation rounds. Throughout the event, Kina provides conversation prompts, secret missions, countdown timers, and partner information while intentionally minimizing screen time.

The application is designed to support organizers without requiring constant facilitation, enabling meetups to run naturally with minimal intervention.

Problem Statement

Meeting new people is often awkward, especially when participants do not know each other.

Traditional meetups frequently rely on unstructured conversations, causing:

- people to remain in familiar groups,
- quieter participants to be overlooked,
- awkward introductions,
- uneven participation,
- and difficulty maintaining engagement without a facilitator.

Most social applications solve this problem by increasing digital interaction.

Kina takes the opposite approach.

Technology should encourage real conversations, not replace them.

Solution

Kina provides lightweight guidance throughout a meetup by:

- assigning conversation activities,
- generating random conversation prompts,
- providing secret missions,
- helping participants pair with new people,
- synchronizing activities through shared timers,
- and allowing organizers to monitor the event with minimal involvement.

Once participants receive their instructions, the application encourages them to lock their phones and focus entirely on the people around them.

Core Principles
1. People Before Phones

Every interaction should last only a few seconds.

The goal is to encourage face-to-face conversation rather than prolonged phone use.

2. Simplicity

Every screen should have one primary purpose.

Participants should never wonder what to do next.

3. Calm Design

The application avoids unnecessary animations, notifications, and distractions.

The interface supports the meetup instead of competing with it.

4. Human Connection

Success is measured by the quality of conversations, not the amount of time users spend inside the application.

Target Users

Kina is intended for:

- community meetups,
- networking events,
- university clubs,
- social gatherings,
- coworking communities,
- hobby groups,
- volunteer organizations.

MVP Scope

The first release of Kina will support:

Participants
- Join an event using a QR code
- Enter a display name
- View the current activity
- Reveal a secret mission
- Pair with another participant
- Receive conversation prompts
- View activity timers
- Participate in reflection rounds
- Finish the meetup
Organizer
- Start an event
- Pause an event
- Advance activities
- Monitor participant pairings
- View current activity status
- End the meetup
- Out of Scope

The MVP will not include:

- user accounts,
- authentication,
- messaging,
- friend requests,
- chat,
- notifications,
- event discovery,
- social feeds,
- profiles,
- payments,
- analytics dashboards.

These may be considered in future releases.
